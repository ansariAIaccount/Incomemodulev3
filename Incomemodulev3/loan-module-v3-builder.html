Australian (AASB) Treatment

AASB 9 / 13 / 15 / 124 AASB-specific fields visible only when AASB framework is selected
Key AASB differences from IFRS: AASB is largely converged with IFRS but adds tier-based disclosure (AASB 1053), ASIC lodgement obligations, franking credit tracking on equity income, AASB 124 related-party rules, and APRA prudential reporting for ADIs (Authorised Deposit-Taking Institutions). For non-ADIs and corporate borrowers, treatment is substantively identical to IFRS 9 / 13 / 15.
Priority order if unspecified: Override → PRICE → Generic → Custom. IRR/XIRR removed per spec (Folder 1). IFRS / ASPE deals use the standard bisection solver and ignore this control.

Tier 1 Transtypes — Loan Lifecycle Events

Mid-period Purchase · Prepayment · Penalty · Default · Write-off · Recovery · Cure · Forbearance · Cap. Costs
Mid-Period Purchase no trade accrual Accrued-but-unpaid interest paid to seller at trade date — booked as receivable, clears on next coupon
Prepayment (voluntary) no prepayment Voluntary early repayment — penalty (if any) auto-applied from #5 schedule below
Prepayment Penalty Schedule no penalty schedule Time-banded penalty rate (e.g. 2.0% Yr1, 1.0% Yr2, 0% thereafter)
Default Interest & Default Fee no default event Covenant breach triggers penalty rate (+bps) and a one-off default fee
Write-Off (Stage 3 derecognition) no write-off Allowance absorbs first, residual hits P&L · IFRS 9 §5.4.4 / ASC 326-20-35-8
Recovery (post-write-off) no recovery Cash recovered after write-off — reverses prior impairment expense
Cure (Stage 2→1 or 3→2) no cure Borrower returns to performing — release ECL allowance to P&L
Forbearance (Payment Holiday) no forbearance Payment holiday — defers expected interest into 40100 reclass
Capitalised Origination Costs no capitalised cost Legal / transaction / valuation costs paid at signing — capitalised into 141000 per IFRS 9 §B5.4 / ASC 310-20-25-2
swap_horiz

Tier 2 Transtypes — Disposal · Restructuring · Timing

Loan Sale · Participation · D4E · Mandatory Prepay · Amort Profile · Trade Date · Multiple events supported where it makes sense
Loan Sale (Full Derecognition) no sale event IFRS 9 §3.2.3 / ASC 860 — derecognise entire loan, recognise gain/loss on disposal
Loan Participation (Partial Sell-Down) no participation IFRS 9 §3.2.6 — fully proportionate share transfer test
Debt-for-Equity Swap no D4E swap IFRIC 19 / ASC 470-50-40 — extinguish loan, recognise equity at FV, restructuring loss
Mandatory Prepayment (Covenant-Triggered) no mandatory prepay Change-of-control · excess cash flow · asset sale · IPO · insurance — usually waives penalty
Amortisation Profile bullet (default) Engine auto-generates the principalSchedule from the profile spec
Profile expands at buildSchedule() entry; only fires when principalSchedule[] has no explicit paydowns. User-supplied schedules always win.
Period-End Reversing Entries disabled Standard month-end-close pattern — accrual JEs auto-reverse on day 1 of next period
When enabled, the engine scans each accrual JE posted on summary.periodEnd (interest, PIK, fee, default int/fee, EIR accretion, non-use fee) and emits a flipped DR/CR twin on day 1 of the next period with a Reversing — prefix. The cash receipt JE then books the full amount to income in the new period.
Trade vs Settlement Date Accounting settlement-date (default) IFRS 9 §B3.1.5 — recognise asset on trade date or settlement date
When method = trade-date AND tradeDate < settle: engine emits a pre-recognition pair (DR 141000 / CR 232000) on trade date and its reversal on settle. Net asset balance after settlement is unchanged.
Hedge De-Designation no de-designation IFRS 9 §6.5.6 — discontinue hedge accounting; OCI amortises to P&L over remaining life
Requires the deal to have a hedge designated. Engine freezes new OCI accumulation and starts daily straight-line amortisation of the existing CFH reserve from this date through maturity. Reclass JEs land in the dedicated "Hedge Reserve Reclass — De-Designation Amortisation" transtype.
CFH Reclassification Transtype n/a — no hedge on deal Read-only — splits CFH reserve recycling from raw coupon income in P&L disclosure
When this deal's hedge reserve is reclassified to P&L, the JE lands on a dedicated transtype that distinguishes "CFH Reserve Recycling" (settlement-driven, IFRS 9 §6.5.11) from "Hedge Reserve Reclass — De-Designation Amortisation" (post de-designation, IFRS 9 §6.5.6). Both route to 421000 but with separate transtype labels for IFRS 7 §24A-B disclosures.
FX Hedge of Loan Principal n/a — no FXP hedge on deal IFRS 9 §6.5.16(c) — OCI routes to FX Hedge Reserve (370000), basis to Cost of Hedging (375000)
When this deal's hedge has type: 'FXP' or subType: 'fxPrincipal', the OCI emission routes to a dedicated FX Hedge Reserve account instead of the generic CFH Reserve (360000). The JE label includes "(FX Principal)" so reports can isolate FX-hedge OCI from interest-rate-hedge OCI.
Allowance Reversal — Model Recalibration no model reversal Same mechanic as cure (#10) — distinct transtype for IFRS 7 §35F ECL roll-forward
Recovery Allocation Buckets no allocated recovery IFRS 7 §35K — split recovery cash between principal / default int / default fee / legal
Revolver Origination Cost Deferral maturity basis (default) ASC 310-20-25-19 — revolver origination costs amortise over commitment period, not maturity
When basis = commitment AND availabilityEnd is set, the engine emits a memo JE on settlement documenting the policy choice. The actual amortisation flows through the existing EIR mechanism — capitalised costs into 141000 carrying still happen on day 1 (#12), but reports show the shorter amort period.
PIK Interest (contractual override) disabled Edits flow into the engine on next Run Accounting
When enabled, daily PIK accrues at the chosen rate and capitalises into 141000 Investments at Cost on the chosen frequency, posted as Investment accretion - PIK interest.

Accounting — Change Handling & Audit

Deal amendments flow through cleanly with full historical traceability
Modifications & re-runs
Late-identified rate changes, restructures, expected-cashflow updates trigger controlled re-calculations. Original entries preserved; new entries dated and reasoned.
Gain/loss treatment
Modification gain/loss recognised where required; derecognition handled as a separate event. New EIR computed when terms substantially change.
Period-on-period explainability
Variance from prior period decomposes into rate, balance, days, and modification effects — all drillable to the underlying deal version.
Sample journal export
Per event: debit/credit, account mapping, entity, currency, dates, deal/event ID, run metadata (date, effective date, inputs, version).
Audit re-performance
